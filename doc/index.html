Persona

This persona illustrates the lived experience behind rural healthcare access challenges in Mills County, Iowa. It provides context for interpreting dashboard insights and grounding the analysis in human-centered design.

About This Persona

Personas help bring data to life. These fictional profiles are grounded in real experiences and represent a blend of many different stories. Personas are a way to help decision-makers understand communities’ needs or how a real individual might interact with services based on their life circumstances, and where additional resources may be needed.

Project Write-Up

This project expands on the themes I introduced in my earlier myth-busting assignment, where I argued that rural health myths cannot be meaningfully understood without context. One of the most effective ways to bring that context into focus is through data. To explore the relationships between geography, access, and health outcomes in rural Iowa, I used RStudio to retrieve and prepare Census data, which informed the maps and indicators used in this dashboard. I also incorporated data from the SDOH and Place Project Database, including datasets from the CDC, to ground the analysis in well-established public health metrics. Recent political developments have prompted me to think more critically about the indirect externalities associated with the closure or consolidation of rural hospitals, an issue that is becoming increasingly pressing across the state. As someone who already drives a significant distance daily, I am wildly aware of how quickly fuel costs, time, and vehicle maintenance can add up to a significant burden. For residents who are financially strained or juggling multiple responsibilities, needing to travel further for medical care may push basic routine care even further out of reach. This may lead to delays in preventive screenings, follow-up care, and early intervention that can quickly escalate into more severe and costly health outcomes over time. Iowa is currently experiencing rapidly rising cancer rates and facing numerous other environmental and health challenges, such as water pollution and binge drinking rates. Given these concerns, all Iowans, both rural and urban, should be concerned about the broader consequences of losing rural healthcare. To help articulate these concerns, I created a persona based on several real individuals from rural communities who have shared their experiences of aging, caregiving, and navigating the healthcare system. Personas can be a powerful tool because they translate complex statistics into relatable stories, helping communities better understand their own data and make informed, data-driven decisions to strengthen local well-being and vitality.
